🎬 Cinematic B-Roll

the extra little shots that make a video feel finished

my notes from this video

  • B-roll is basically all the extra footage that isn't the "main" shot — close-ups, details, little moments
  • Explains that mixing up shot sizes (a wide one, then closer ones) is what makes stuff feel put-together
  • Says you don't need fancy gear, just grabbing a handful of small detail shots in whatever you're filming
  • Also gets into how it gets used later when editing, which is helpful context

Simple stuff to remember:

  • Moving the camera slowly (or not at all) tends to look way more "cinematic" than fast movement
  • This lens is actually really good for close-up detail shots since it can focus pretty close on small things
  • Try grabbing a few extra random shots any time you're filming something — you'll be glad you have them when editing
