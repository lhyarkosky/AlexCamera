🛋️ Indoor Interviews

sitting someone down and filming them talking, inside

my notes from this video

  • Basically about making a normal, kind of boring room look nicer just by where you put the camera and the person
  • Turns out where you sit someone relative to a window or lamp matters a lot more than I expected
  • Talks about simple lighting — even one light plus something to bounce light back onto the person's face helps
  • Good if you're just filming in a living room or bedroom and don't have "real" equipment

Simple stuff to remember:

  • Try to face the person toward a window if there is one — natural light is the easiest way to look good
  • Indoor rooms can sound echoey on camera — getting the mic closer to the person helps a lot
  • This lens is nice and tight for one person's face, so as long as you can back up a bit in the room you should be good
