🌤️ Outdoor Interviews

same idea as indoor, but outside

my notes from this video

  • Talks about where to put the person relative to the sun so they don't end up squinting or in harsh shadows
  • Overcast/cloudy days apparently make lighting way easier outside — soft light everywhere instead of harsh sun
  • Mentions using a reflector to throw some light back onto the person's face
  • Also brings up outdoor audio — wind is the enemy, worth having something on the mic to block wind noise

Simple stuff to remember:

  • Shooting super early morning or later in the afternoon tends to look nicer than the middle of the day
  • If it's really bright and sunny, don't stress about it looking "too washed out" — that's a normal problem, just try to find some shade
  • Keep an eye on wind messing with audio if you're using an external mic
