🏃 Handheld Run & Gun

fast, moving, no-tripod filming

my notes from this video

  • This is about just filming while walking around/moving, holding the camera instead of using a tripod
  • Covers how you hold and brace the camera against your body to keep it steadier
  • Mentions how your own footsteps/walking style affects how shaky the footage looks
  • A good starting point before worrying about any stabilizer gear

Simple stuff to remember:

  • There should be a stabilization setting in the camera's video menu — worth turning on for this kind of shooting
  • Walking slowly and smoothly (almost like tip-toeing) makes a bigger difference than you'd think
  • This lens being kind of "zoomed in" makes it a little trickier moving around in small spaces — just something to be aware of
